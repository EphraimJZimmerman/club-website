The Brandeis University Quant Club is a charted club at Brandeis University.


Who we are

The Brandeis University Quant Club is an undergraduate education and research club founded in 2021 aimed to educate members on How to Think Quantitatively. Our overall intentions are to bestow valuable insights, conceptual knowledge, and meaningful experiences to students interested in software engineering, applied mathematics, and quantitative finance. You will:


  • Learn the fundamentals of the global financial markets, including foreign exchange, U.S. and foreign equities, fixed-income and derivative pricing.

  • Have the opportunity to build large-scale application in JavaScript and Python.

  • Get introduced to advanced quantitative finance topics, including but not limited to numerical methods, probability, and stochastic processes.

  • Learn real-world uses of applied mathematics.

Our project (Spr. 2023)

Our 2023 spring semester project entails designing a platform that quantifies risk and viability for an individual’s equity and or option-based portfolio using various metrics and ratios, such as the Sharpe, Sortino, Treynor, Sterling, and Calmar in addition to the fundamental variables involved in their mechanics, (i.e., the greeks).
