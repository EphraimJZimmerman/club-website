The Quantitative Finance & Modeling Club is a chartered club at Brandeis University.


Who we are

The Brandeis University Quantitative Finance & Modeling Club, or “Quant Club” is an undergraduate education and research club founded in 2021 aimed to educate members on How to Think Quantitatively. Our overall intentions are to bestow valuable insights, conceptual knowledge, and meaningful experiences to students interested in software engineering, data science, applied mathematics, and (of course) quantitative finance. You will:


  • Learn the fundamentals of the global financial markets, including foreign exchange, U.S. and foreign equities, fixed-income and derivative pricing.

  • Understand GitHub and project workflows

  • Get introduced to advanced quantitative finance topics, including but not limited to numerical methods, fundamentals of how probability is used in models, and an introduction to neural networks and A.I. with regard to the financial markets.

  • Develop advanced programming skills in Python and JavaScript

  • Work collaboratively on semester projects

Our projects

In spring 2022, we developed a project to teach members how to 'contextualize and group arbitrary data' with JavaScript and React. Named R-TCI, or the Real-Time climate index, members were asked to parse research done on a specific area to measure climate change, subsequently using that data to create a range from "okay" to "detrimental". As a team, we then compiled this data into a single index between 1-100. In other words, we solved the problem: "how do we take arbitrary data and create context for it?"
